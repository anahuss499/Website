Azkar & Duas

Common daily Azkar and short dua selections with references. Arabic is provided; a brief translation and a classical reference are listed for each item.

Morning Azkar

  1. سُبْحَانَ اللَّهِ

    Translation: Glory be to Allah. Ref: Frequently used dhikr (general).

  2. الْحَمْدُ لِلَّهِ

    Translation: All praise is due to Allah. Ref: General adhkar.

  3. اللَّهُ أَكْبَرُ

    Translation: Allah is the Greatest. Ref: General dhikr.

  4. أَصْبَحْتُ وَأَصْبَحَ المُـلْكُ لِلَّهِ

    Translation: I have entered the morning and at this very time all dominion belongs to Allah. Ref: Hadith collections (morning supplication).

Evening Azkar

  1. بِسْمِ اللَّهِ الَّذِي لا يَضُرُّ مَعَ اسْمِهِ شَيْءٌ

    Translation: In the name of Allah with whose name nothing can harm. Ref: Reported in at‑Tirmidhi and others.

  2. حَسْبِيَ اللَّهُ لا إِلَهَ إِلا هُوَ

    Translation: Allah is sufficient for me; there is no deity except Him. Ref: Mentioned in various hadith.

Short Duas (Everyday)

  • رَبَّنَا آتِنَا فِي الدُّنْيَا حَسَنَةً وَفِي الآخِرَةِ حَسَنَةً

    Translation: Our Lord, grant us good in this world and in the Hereafter. Ref: Quran (Al-Baqarah).

  • اللَّهُمَّ إِنِّي أَعُوذُ بِكَ مِنْ الْهَمِّ وَالْحَزَنِ

    Translation: O Allah, I seek refuge in You from worry and sadness. Ref: Common supplication.

For complete collections and longer versions, recommended references include: Hisn al‑Muslim (Fortress of the Muslim), Sahih collections and classical books of supplication.
